# Five Football App Ideas That Can Actually Make Money
### Deep global market research — July 2026

This report is based on live research across the global football app economy (July 2026): company filings, funding announcements, app-intelligence reports (AppTweak, Sensor Tower), monetization benchmark reports (RevenueCat, Adapty, Playwire, MonetizeMore), pricing pages, and case studies. Every important claim has a source link. The five ideas at the end were selected specifically for **low competition + high value + realistic to start now with a small team**.

---

## Part 1 — What the research proved about how football apps make money

Before the ideas, here is the hard reality the data shows. This is what separates a plan that works from a fantasy.

**1. The famous categories are brutally saturated — do not enter them head-on.**
Live score apps are owned by giants: Flashscore has 155M+ monthly users and ~€350M revenue ([Flashscore](https://www.flashscore.com/news/flashscore-celebrates-155-million-monthly-users-400-million-app-downloads/zkFjk8kA/), [Livesport filings](https://grokipedia.com/page/Livesport)); FotMob has 20M+ monthly users and ~$14M revenue with only ~30 staff ([Forbes](https://www.forbes.com/sites/manasipathak-1/2025/08/09/how-fotmob-app-went-from-a-honeymoon-idea-to-20-million-monthly-users/), [Josimar](https://josimarfootball.com/2026/04/30/in-through-the-back-door/)); SofaScore, 365Scores (bought by gambling giant Entain for $160M — [Ctech](https://www.calcalistech.com/ctechnews/article/rj1jek9113)) and LiveScore fill the rest. General football news/content is shrinking: even GOAL's owner Footballco saw traffic fall from ~90M to ~63M monthly visits because of Google AI Overviews ([Press Gazette](https://pressgazette.co.uk/media_business/footballco-2025-revenue-world-cup-juan-delgado-interview/)). A new generic score or news app will die.

**2. Ads only work at huge scale — unless your audience is rich-country or you stack affiliate on top.**
Real 2026 numbers: blended ad eCPMs are roughly **US $5–12 per 1,000 impressions vs Nigeria $0.35, India $0.34, Indonesia $0.20** ([MonetizeMore](https://www.monetizemore.com/blog/how-much-ad-revenue-can-apps-generate/), [country CPM data](https://www.thesrzone.com/2024/01/admob-ecpm-rates-by-country.html)). With a mostly US/UK audience you need only ~9,000 daily active users for ~$10k/month; with a mostly African/South-Asian audience you need ~185,000 DAU for the same money ([Playwire benchmarks](https://www.playwire.com/blog/admob-ecpm-benchmarks-what-publishers-should-expect)). Rewarded video pays best ($10–30 eCPM); banners are near-worthless.

**3. Betting-affiliate money is 10–50x bigger per user than ads or merch.**
Bookmakers pay **$50–300 CPA per first-time depositor (up to $500 in the US)** or 25–45% lifetime revenue share ([AffPapa](https://affpapa.com/best-sports-betting-affiliate-programs/), [OddsPapi](https://oddspapi.io/blog/monetize-sports-odds-data/)). This is why every score app is stuffed with betting ads and why a betting-affiliate company paid ~€105M for FUTBIN ([Sportico](https://www.sportico.com/business/tech/2025/futbin-ea-sports-third-party-rights-1234852814/)). Use it carefully (web is far more permissive than app stores for this) — but do not ignore it.

**4. Subscriptions are hard but the winners charge more than you'd think.**
80.8% of subscription apps never reach $1,000/month; only 5.3% reach $10k/month ([RevenueCat data](https://www.start.io/blog/report-80-of-mobile-apps-fail-to-earn-1000-month-in-subscription-revenue/)). Median US pricing is $15.20/month ([Adapty 2025](https://adapty.io/blog/state-of-in-app-subscriptions-2025-in-10-minutes/)). The lesson: don't sell a $2 subscription to poor-fit casual fans; sell a $15–40/month subscription to someone with a real problem (a coach, a team, a parent, a serious player). Hard paywalls earn 8x more per install than freemium ([RevenueCat 2026](https://www.revenuecat.com/blog/growth/subscription-app-trends-benchmarks-2026/)).

**5. It's cheap to run a football app in 2026.**
Football data APIs from **$19–39/month** (API-Football) or free tiers ([api-football](https://www.api-football.com/pricing), [football-data.org](https://www.football-data.org/pricing)); hosting $10–50/month; Apple $99/yr, Google $25 once; Apple/Google take only 15% under $1M revenue. Total fixed cost: **~$50–180/month**. The barrier is distribution and product, not money.

**6. Small teams DO win in football — in niches, and fast.**
FotMob: 2 brothers, bootstrapped, now ~$19M/yr ([Forbes](https://www.forbes.com/sites/manasipathak-1/2025/08/09/how-fotmob-app-went-from-a-honeymoon-idea-to-20-million-monthly-users/)). Immaculate Grid: one developer, built in 2 days, 100,000+ daily players in 3 months, sold to Sports Reference on day 100 ([Action Network](https://www.actionnetwork.com/mlb/the-story-behind-immaculate-grid-the-game-thats-captivated-baseball-fans)). 3AK Track: two 19-year-olds, no-code AI sports training app, **$45k in 2 months** ([MRR Story](https://www.mrrstory.com/stories/this-no-code-sports-app-hit-45k-in-2-months)). Fantasy Football Scout charges £50/yr just for tools on top of the free FPL game ([FFS pricing](https://www.fantasyfootballscout.co.uk/pricing)).

**7. The 2026 World Cup just lit a fire under the market.**
Sports app downloads hit a record 137M in Q2 2026; FotMob's weekly downloads tripled during the tournament ([Sensor Tower Sports Report 2026](https://sensortower.com/blog/beyond-the-game-the-sports-report-2026)). MLS viewership is up 62% in 2026 ([MLS](https://www.mlssoccer.com/news/major-league-soccer-viewership-up-62-in-2026-as-fan-growth-continues-across-platforms)). The US is now the single biggest new-football-fan opportunity in the world, and those fans pay Tier-1 ad rates.

## Part 2 — The Five Ideas

Ranked by the combination of low competition, willingness to pay, and speed to first revenue.

---

### IDEA 1 — "Veo without the $1,600 camera": AI match highlights & stats from a phone, for grassroots teams

**What it is.** A mobile app where a Sunday-league / youth / academy team puts one phone on a cheap tripod at the halfway line, records the match, and the app's AI automatically produces: a highlights reel, individual player clips (for each kid/player), basic stats (possession zones, shots, goals), and a shareable match page for parents and players. Teams pay a monthly subscription.

**The problem and proof of demand.** Amateur teams desperately want match video — it's the #1 growing spend in youth football — but today's solutions are absurdly expensive: a Veo camera costs **$1,599 hardware + $1,599/year subscription** (realistic 3-year cost $9,000+), Trace costs a 15-player team **~$2,400/year and you never own the equipment**, Reeplayer is $1,499 + $1,188/yr ([Reeplayer buyer's guide](https://www.reeplayer.com/blog/youth-soccer-camera-comparison-2025-reeplayer-vs-veo-vs-trace-complete-buyer-s-guide), [Veo comparison](https://www.veo.com/article/soccer-camera-systems-compared)). The fact that thousands of amateur clubs pay these prices anyway is the proof of demand. Users also complain about 12–24 hour cloud processing delays. Meanwhile the phone in every coach's pocket now shoots 4K, and on-device/cloud AI video analysis has become cheap.

**Why competition is low.** The incumbents are hardware companies protecting hardware margins — they won't cannibalize themselves with a phone-only product. The phone-based challengers (e.g., once.football) are small and mostly Europe-focused. There is no dominant "just use your phone" player. This is a classic disruption gap: same job, 10x cheaper.

**How it makes money.** Team subscription at **$20–40/month per team** (vs $130+/month equivalent for Veo — an easy sell). A team has 15–20 players/parents, so per-family cost is ~$1–2/month. Upsells: individual player highlight accounts for recruiting ($5/month, parents pay), club-wide plans ($100–200/month). At benchmark subscription economics this is a high-ticket B2B-ish product, exactly the kind RevenueCat data says wins ([RevenueCat 2026](https://www.revenuecat.com/blog/growth/subscription-app-trends-benchmarks-2026/)). **500 teams × $30/month = $15k/month. 5,000 teams = $150k/month.** There are hundreds of thousands of organized amateur/youth teams in the US, UK and Europe alone.

**What it costs.** Build: a solid MVP needs mobile recording + upload + an AI pipeline (player/ball detection, event spotting, auto-clipping — open-source models like YOLO-family + tracking get you far). This is the most technically demanding of the five ideas; realistic MVP is 3–6 months for a small dev team, or start "manual-AI hybrid" (AI-assisted human clipping) to launch in weeks. Running costs: cloud GPU processing per match (~$0.50–2/match) — priced into the subscription.

**Start tomorrow.** (1) Post in youth-club Facebook groups / Reddit coaching communities offering free AI highlights for 10 pilot teams if they film with a phone; (2) process the first matches semi-manually with existing AI tools to validate willingness to pay before writing heavy code; (3) charge from month one ($20/month founding-team price). The 2026 World Cup just sent US youth soccer registration surging — timing is perfect ([Sensor Tower](https://sensortower.com/blog/beyond-the-game-the-sports-report-2026)).

### IDEA 2 — Verified digital CV + scouting platform for aspiring players (Africa-first): the anti-scam football passport

**What it is.** A platform (web + Android-first, data-light) where young players build a **verified football CV**: bio, position, verified age, video highlights (can come from Idea 1's pipeline!), match history confirmed by their club/academy, and standardized skill-test videos recorded through the app camera. On the other side: **verified, vetted academies, agents and scouts** pay to search the database and contact players. Verification badges both ways are the core product.

**The problem and proof of demand.** This is the highest-stakes gap found in the entire research. Thousands of young African players are scammed or even trafficked every year by fake agents charging families $3,000+ for fake trials ([The Conversation](https://theconversation.com/human-traffickers-are-using-football-dreams-to-lure-young-ghanaian-men-to-nigeria-how-to-stop-it-277536), [ISS Africa](https://issafrica.org/iss-today/going-for-gold-africas-young-footballers-exploited-by-smugglers), [Sports & Crime](https://www.sportsandcrime.com/p/fake-agents-traffic-thousands-of)). The demand side (millions of talented young players desperate for legitimate exposure) is enormous and currently served by scammers because **no trusted platform exists**. Clubs like Chelsea and Burnley already use AI scouting app aiScout to find talent remotely — proving pro clubs will scout via app. Existing "player CV" sites ([playerprofile.io](https://playerprofile.io/), [globalfootballplatform.com](https://www.globalfootballplatform.com/profile-player-page/)) are tiny, generic, and have no verification layer — that's the whole game.

**Why competition is low.** It's a trust problem, not a tech problem, so no one has cracked it; big scouting tech (Wyscout, StatsPerform) only covers professional leagues; the CV sites are unverified directories. An Africa-first, verification-first product has essentially an open field — and network effects make the winner very hard to displace.

**How it makes money.** Three stacked streams: (1) **Freemium player side** — free basic profile; premium profile (more videos, featured placement, downloadable PDF CV) at $1–3/month priced for the market, paid via mobile money; even 100k premium players × $2 = $200k/month at scale; (2) **Scout/academy subscriptions** — $50–200/month for verified recruiter accounts with database search (the Tier-1-money side); (3) **Verified trial events** with academy partners and sponsors later. Sponsorship potential is huge — betting brands, telcos and boot brands spend heavily on African football audiences, and free-to-play sports platforms like Superbru already sustain themselves this way ([Superbru model](https://grokipedia.com/page/superbru)).

**What it costs.** Technically simple: profiles, video upload, search, payments (Paystack/Flutterwave/M-Pesa) — an MVP in 4–8 weeks. The real work is partnerships: local academies, federations, and a vetting process for recruiters. Running cost <$100/month at start.

**Start tomorrow.** (1) Partner with 3–5 legitimate academies (e.g., in Lagos/Accra) to seed verified players; (2) launch a simple web version with WhatsApp-based onboarding (data-light, where the users already are); (3) get one real success story (a player signed via the platform) and let word of mouth do the rest. Given the trafficking problem, NGOs, FIFA development programs and journalists will amplify a legitimate solution for free.

### IDEA 3 — Free-to-play prediction game for underserved competitions, monetized by sponsors + affiliate (the proven Superbru model, aimed where no one is aiming)

**What it is.** A free prediction/pick'em game (predict scores, streaks, leaderboards, private leagues with friends/offices) — but built for competitions and markets the incumbents ignore: AFCON 2027, the Nigerian NPFL and other African leagues, Copa Libertadores, and the post-World-Cup US soccer fan (MLS). Not fantasy (regulatory pain), not real-money (banned in many places — India killed a $2T industry overnight, [Business Standard](https://www.business-standard.com/companies/news/dream11-rmg-ban-new-strategy-revenue-loss-layoffs-125082600219_1.html)) — pure free-to-play prediction, which is regulation-proof.

**The problem and proof of demand.** Fans everywhere love proving they know football; private-league banter is the retention engine (FPL hit a record **13.1M players** on exactly this psychology, [Premier League](https://www.premierleague.com/en/news/4362780)). Superbru proves the model monetizes at small scale: **2.5M registered players** sustained for years on display ads, tournament sponsorships and betting-affiliate commissions ([Superbru](https://www.superbru.com/about), [CEO interview](https://www.biznews.com/rugby/2025/03/21/superbru-ceo-andy-wood)). Meanwhile prediction-style engagement is the **fastest-growing segment in US sports apps** (from <1% to 16% of sports-app users 2023–2026, [Sensor Tower](https://sensortower.com/blog/beyond-the-game-the-sports-report-2026)).

**Why competition is low.** Superbru is rugby/UK-centric and dated; nobody has built the polished prediction game for African leagues, AFCON, or the new US soccer fan. Local leagues have passionate fans and literally zero good digital products. Sponsors (betting brands, telcos, banks, breweries) are desperate for exactly this audience and have few native places to spend.

**How it makes money.** (1) **Sponsorship** — sell branded tournaments/"megapools" to companies (Superbru's core revenue: custom theming, brand exposure, opt-in email data); one sponsor at $2–10k per tournament, several tournaments a year; (2) **Betting affiliate** — a prediction audience is the perfect affiliate audience: $50–150 CPA per referred depositor in Africa/EU, $100–500 in the US ([AffPapa](https://affpapa.com/best-sports-betting-affiliate-programs/)) — even 50 referrals/month = $2.5–7.5k/month; keep this on the web side to avoid app-store gambling rules ([platform policy guide](https://gmelawyers.com/navigating-platform-policies-3-google-apple/)); (3) display ads as the base layer.

**What it costs.** The cheapest build of the five: fixtures/results from API-Football at **$19–39/month** ([pricing](https://www.api-football.com/pricing)), a web app first (SEO + WhatsApp sharing), MVP in 3–6 weeks. Total running cost <$100/month.

**Start tomorrow.** (1) Build the web MVP around one competition with a rabid fan base and zero competition (e.g., NPFL season or AFCON qualifiers); (2) seed private leagues through WhatsApp groups, offices, campus communities; (3) approach one betting brand or telco for a season sponsorship once you have 5–10k players. Launch free, grow through the private-league invite loop.

### IDEA 4 — Paid AI tools ecosystem on top of free fantasy games — aimed at the new US soccer fan (MLS Fantasy) and beyond

**What it is.** You don't compete with free official fantasy games — you sell the **tools** their obsessed players crave. An AI assistant app: optimal team picker, transfer/trade advisor, captaincy and fixture-difficulty analysis, price-change predictions, "explain my rank" insights. Start with **MLS Fantasy** (booming audience, zero tool ecosystem), then expand to FPL (huge but competitive), AFCON/World Cup fantasy games, and other leagues.

**The problem and proof of demand.** Fantasy managers are the most tool-hungry users in football. On the back of free FPL, **Fantasy Football Scout charges £50/year** and has done so profitably for years ([pricing](https://www.fantasyfootballscout.co.uk/pricing)); Fantasy Football Hub sells AI team-pickers the same way. That entire paid ecosystem exists for FPL only. Meanwhile MLS viewership is up **62% in 2026** ([MLS](https://www.mlssoccer.com/news/major-league-soccer-viewership-up-62-in-2026-as-fan-growth-continues-across-platforms)), the World Cup just converted millions of Americans into weekly soccer fans ([YouGov](https://yougov.com/en-us/articles/54989-fifa-world-cup-2026-is-already-outscoring-the-2022-edition-in-the-us), [Front Office Sports](https://frontofficesports.com/whats-next-for-soccer-in-the-u-s/)) — and these are **Tier-1 payers** (US median app subscription: $15.20/month, [Adapty](https://adapty.io/blog/state-of-in-app-subscriptions-2025-in-10-minutes/)).

**Why competition is low.** The FPL tools market is crowded, but the identical model applied to MLS Fantasy, and to every other official free fantasy game (Bundesliga, La Liga, UCL Fantasy, AFCON fantasy), is nearly empty. You're arbitraging a proven business model into ignored markets at exactly the moment the US audience explodes.

**How it makes money.** Hard-paywall subscription (**$4.99–9.99/month or $30–50/year**) — remember hard paywalls earn 8x freemium per install and long trials convert at 42.5% ([RevenueCat 2026](https://www.revenuecat.com/blog/growth/subscription-app-trends-benchmarks-2026/)). Just 2,000 subscribers at $5/month = $10k/month — reachable in a niche where you're the only serious tool. Secondary: content/YouTube/newsletter feeding the funnel, plus US sportsbook affiliate ($100–500 CPA) on the web side.

**What it costs.** Data is the main input — official fantasy APIs (FPL's is free/open; MLS data via API-Football/Sportmonks from $19–29/month, [Sportmonks](https://www.sportmonks.com/football-api/plans-pricing/)). The "AI" is optimization + LLM explanations — well within a small team's reach. MVP in 4–8 weeks as a web app (fantasy players live in browsers), then wrap as an app.

**Start tomorrow.** (1) Ship a free MLS Fantasy "team rater" web tool this month to capture SEO ("MLS fantasy picks week X") while the post-World-Cup wave is hot; (2) build an email list from it; (3) launch the paid AI assistant tier before next MLS season. Repeat playbook for UCL Fantasy and AFCON.

### IDEA 5 — A network of daily football puzzle games in ignored languages/markets — the fastest cash + acquisition play

**What it is.** A portfolio of daily football brain games (guess-the-player, career-path, lineup grids, football Wordle-style) — but localized where nobody has gone: **Arabic, Portuguese (Brazil-tuned), Bahasa Indonesia, Swahili, Hindi, French (West Africa)** — plus themed editions the English incumbents ignore (women's football, African legends, MLS for new US fans). Web-first with share-to-WhatsApp emoji results, then app versions with rewarded ads.

**The problem and proof of demand.** Daily football games are proven traffic machines: Futbol11 gets ~3M visits/month with an extraordinary 10 pages per visit ([Similarweb](https://www.similarweb.com/website/futbol-11.com/)); "Who Are Ya?" went viral to millions of plays ([Forbes](https://www.forbes.com/sites/mattgardner1/2022/03/09/wordle-football-spin-off-who-are-ya-may-be-the-best-yet/)); Immaculate Grid went from side project to acquisition in 100 days ([Action Network](https://www.actionnetwork.com/mlb/the-story-behind-immaculate-grid-the-game-thats-captivated-baseball-fans)). But the winners are all English/Spanish — the biggest football-mad language markets on earth are unserved.

**Why competition is low.** The existing players are tiny teams with no localization ambition; football knowledge is deeply local (an Indonesian daily game about Liga 1 legends has zero competitors); daily-habit games have strong first-mover lock-in (players keep their streaks where they started).

**How it makes money.** (1) Display/rewarded ads — yes, low eCPM markets, but these games generate 7–10 pageviews per visit daily, and portfolio scale across languages multiplies it; a network doing 10M monthly pageviews at even a $1 blended CPM = $10k/month; (2) sponsorship takeovers during AFCON/World Cup/Copa América; (3) the exit path — sports-data and media companies buy these games for their audiences (Immaculate Grid → Sports Reference; Wordle → NYT for low seven figures). Honest caveat: this is the lowest revenue-per-user idea of the five — its virtue is **speed** (days to build), near-zero cost, and it feeds traffic to Ideas 3 and 4.

**What it costs.** Almost nothing: static web games + a player database (free/cheap via football-data.org or API-Football, [pricing](https://www.football-data.org/pricing)); each new game is a template + new dataset; MVP in 1–2 weeks.

**Start tomorrow.** (1) Pick one language-market with huge football culture and zero incumbents (Brazil-Portuguese or Bahasa Indonesia — SofaScore's #1 market is already Indonesia, proving appetite, [SofaScore](https://www.sofascore.com/news/sofascore-among-top-sports-apps-in-2026)); (2) clone the proven mechanics with local league content; (3) seed in local football Twitter/X, TikTok and WhatsApp; (4) reinvest traffic into the prediction game (Idea 3).

## Part 3 — How the five fit together, and what I'd do first

| # | Idea | Time to launch | Cost to run | Revenue ceiling | Competition | Who pays |
|---|------|---------------|-------------|----------------|-------------|----------|
| 1 | Phone-AI match highlights | 3–6 months (or weeks hybrid) | Medium (GPU per match) | Very high ($100k+/mo) | **Very low** | Teams/clubs/parents (Tier-1) |
| 2 | Verified player CV + scouting | 1–2 months | Very low | Very high + network effects | **Very low** | Players (volume) + scouts (Tier-1) |
| 3 | Prediction game (Superbru model) | 3–6 weeks | Very low | Medium-high | **Low** in target markets | Sponsors + affiliates |
| 4 | Fantasy AI tools (MLS-first) | 1–2 months | Very low | High ($10k+/mo realistic) | **Low** outside FPL | US/EU fans (Tier-1 subs) |
| 5 | Localized daily games network | 1–2 weeks | Near zero | Low-medium + exit potential | **Low** in target languages | Advertisers + acquirer |

**The smart sequence:** start with **Idea 5 or 3 for speed** (revenue and audience within weeks, almost no cost), use that audience and cash flow to fund **Idea 4** (first serious subscription revenue from Tier-1 payers), and build toward **Ideas 1 and 2** as the big companies — they have the highest value, the deepest moats, and they even feed each other (Idea 1's highlight clips are exactly what Idea 2's player CVs need).

One caution as you plan: none of this is guaranteed money — the same research shows 80% of subscription apps never clear $1k/month. What tilts the odds here is that each idea (a) copies a monetization model already proven by a named company with real revenue, and (b) aims it at a market or audience the incumbents demonstrably ignore. Execution, distribution and speed decide the rest.

*Note: this is market research, not financial or legal advice — betting-affiliate and youth-data (COPPA/GDPR) aspects need proper legal review in your target countries before launch.*
